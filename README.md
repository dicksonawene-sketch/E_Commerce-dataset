- Orders recorded in different districts and formats  
- Product category names not standardized  
- Missing or NULL reference keys  
- Duplicate or misaligned records across tables  

These inconsistencies required me to **clean, adapt, and validate** all data before meaningful analysis could occur.

## The Analysis

### Query 1 ‚Äì Customer Purchase Overview
**Goal:** Establish how customers and orders relate  
**Why:** Correct joins are the foundation for all later analysis

### Query 2 ‚Äì Revenue Aggregation
**Goal:** Compute total and average revenue  
**Why:** Provides financial baseline for trend analysis

### Query 3 ‚Äì Monthly Revenue Trends
**Goal:** Track revenue over time  
**Why:** Detect growth, decline, and seasonality

### Query 4 ‚Äì Product Category Performance
**Goal:** Compare revenue across categories  
**Why:** Identify top-performing and underperforming categories

### Query 5 ‚Äì Category A vs Category B Customers
**Goal:** Identify customers who bought Category A but not Category B  
**Why:** Demonstrates targeted segmentation and anti-join logic

### Query 6 ‚Äì Repeat Purchase & Retention
**Goal:** Track repeat purchases over time  
**Why:** Measures customer retention and long-term value

### Query 7 ‚Äì Potential Revenue Analysis
**Goal:** Estimate unrealized revenue opportunities  
**Why:** Provides insights for business growth and cross-selling

### Query 8 ‚Äì Delivery Performance
**Goal:** Classify orders as Early, On-Time, or Late  
**Why:** Links logistics performance to customer experience

### Query 9 ‚Äì Delivery Status Distribution
**Goal:** Measure frequency of each delivery outcome  
**Why:** Highlights operational risks

### Query 10 ‚Äì Integrated Business Summary
**Goal:** Combine insights across revenue, customers, and delivery  
**Why:** Produces a clear, business-ready overview
